What is CSWIM?

The CloudWire Solar Weather Impact Model (CSWIM) is an enhancement to traditional weather forecasting. It integrates solar-terrestrial dynamics such as coronal mass ejections (CMEs), solar wind, and geomagnetic storms into atmospheric risk assessment. CSWIM is supported by two companion theories:

  • CSWDT – Consolidated Solar-Wind Discharge Theory
  • ACE – Atmospheric Capacitor Effect

The Science Behind CSWIM

🌞 Solar Activity → Earth’s Magnetosphere

Solar storms inject energy into Earth’s magnetosphere, altering atmospheric circulation.

⚡ CSWDT: Solar Discharge Mechanism

Stored solar energy releases into the atmosphere, expressed as lightning and storms.

⏱️ ACE: Atmospheric Capacitor Effect

Earth’s atmosphere stores charge during solar events and discharges it days later, explaining storm lags.

Validation Report

CSWIM has been validated against ten case studies. Findings show strong support for solar–lightning coupling and hurricane intensification links, emerging evidence for lagged storm responses, and novel contributions such as the capacitor analogy and cyclone seeding.

CSWIM is a developing theoretical framework. Future work will test the model with additional case studies, refine its predictive capacity, and integrate real-time solar-terrestrial monitoring. Researchers and meteorologists are invited to collaborate.
