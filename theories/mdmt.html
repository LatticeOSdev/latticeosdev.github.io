Overview

The Meyerhoff Dark Matter Theory (MDMT) proposes that dark matter is not a particle-based phenomenon, but an emergent effect of gravitational wave interference. When gravitational waves emitted by massive astrophysical events intersect, they create localized distortions in spacetime — forming zones of gravimetric pressure that behave like mass. This model reframes dark matter as a fluid dynamic property of spacetime, governed by Einstein’s relativity and observed through ripple-like interactions in the cosmic medium.
